Biography

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Texas at San Antonio, where I conduct research in the ASIC Laboratory under the supervision of Prof. Mimi Xie. My research focuses on Tiny AI, intermittent computing, energy harvesting, and low-power IoT systems, particularly for real-world applications in healthcare and embedded intelligence.

I completed my B.Sc. in Computer Engineering at Ferdowsi University of Mashhad and have worked as a research assistant and software engineer in both Iran and the U.S. My experience spans backend development, CI/CD pipelines, RESTful APIs, and cloud-native technologies including Docker, Kubernetes, and NGINX.

My academic work includes peer-reviewed publications on fog computing, SDN controller placement, and secure IoT systems. I was selected as a DAC Young Fellow and won first place in the 2-Minute Video Presentation Contest at DAC 2025 for my research in energy-aware AI systems.

In addition to my research, I serve as a teaching assistant for various undergraduate courses such as Operating Systems, Cloud Computing, and Compiler Design, helping students master core computer science concepts.

Outside of academia, I enjoy reading classic literature and learning new languages. I bring curiosity, discipline, and creativity to both my technical and personal pursuits.
