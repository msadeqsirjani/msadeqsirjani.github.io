Biography

Driven by the belief that technology should serve humanity's greatest challenges, I am pursuing my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of Texas at San Antonio, where I research the intersection of artificial intelligence and sustainable computing in the ASIC Laboratory under Prof. Mimi Xie. My mission is to democratize AI by making it accessible on the smallest, most energy-efficient devices, bringing intelligent healthcare solutions to remote communities and embedding smart capabilities into everyday objects.

My journey began at Ferdowsi University of Mashhad, where I discovered that the greatest innovations emerge from bridging theoretical knowledge with real-world impact. From developing robust backend systems to orchestrating cloud-native architectures with Docker and Kubernetes, I've learned that every line of code is an opportunity to solve meaningful problems and create lasting change.

Recognition as a DAC Young Fellow and winning first place at the DAC 2025 Video Presentation Contest validates not just my research in energy-aware AI systems, but my conviction that young researchers can lead transformative breakthroughs. Through peer-reviewed publications spanning fog computing, SDN optimization, and secure IoT systems, I strive to contribute knowledge that opens new possibilities for future generations.

Teaching the next generation of computer scientists, from Operating Systems to Cloud Computing and Compiler Design, reminds me daily that our greatest impact comes through empowering others. Each student I mentor carries forward the potential to solve problems we haven't yet imagined, multiplying the reach of our collective efforts.

Beyond algorithms and architectures, I find inspiration in classic literature and the beauty of human languages, understanding that true innovation requires both technical precision and creative vision. Every challenge is an opportunity to learn, grow, and contribute to a future where technology amplifies human potential rather than replacing it.
